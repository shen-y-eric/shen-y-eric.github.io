chromesthesia

Chromesthesia or sound-to-color synesthesia is a type of synesthesia in which sound involuntarily evokes an experience of color, shape, and movement.

Alexander Scriabin was a Russian composer and pianist who was widely regarded as a synesthete, but there is controversy over whether he specifically had chromesthesia. Nonetheless, Scriabin created a color organ and had a table for sound-to-color mapping. You can try it out with the piano below.
